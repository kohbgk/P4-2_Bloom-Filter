Why two correct Bloom filters set different bits

Each press hashes a URL with real SHA-256, splits the digest into h1 and h2, and walks the k probe positions (h1 + i·h2) mod m through both implementations. GPT keeps h2 as-is; Claude forces it odd with OR 1. Watch what happens when the raw h2 is even.

The array is shrunk to m = 32 bits with k = 4 probes so the stepping is visible; the real scripts use millions of bits and identical arithmetic. Both filters always agree on the first probe (h1 mod m). Roughly half of all SHA-256 digests have an even upper half, so over many URLs about half diverge — which is why the two scripts report different specific false positives while both converging on the same target false-positive rate.
